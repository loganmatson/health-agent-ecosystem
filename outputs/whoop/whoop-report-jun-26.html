Across the window, deep sleep held steady (~98 min) while sleep debt fell 13.7% post-marathon (77.5 → 69.4 min). 03 · Training

Dose & modality

Does strain predict next-day recovery, and does it matter what kind of training? The honest answer: weakly, and no.

Strain → next-day recovery

Each point = one day · quadratic fit overlaid

Recovery by training modality

Next-day recovery mean ± SD by activity type

The quadratic (inverted-U) term is not significant in any period (p=0.20 overall) — there's no clean "optimal strain zone" in this data. A weak negative linear trend holds (r=−0.215, p=0.0095): higher-strain days are followed by slightly lower recovery. No training modality produced a statistically significant next-day difference (all p>0.05). HRV shows a consistent but non-significant gradient — Rest > Other > Strength > Running — hinting that running suppresses HRV more acutely than lifting.

07 · About

Methodology

How this report was built and what it can — and can't — claim.

Sample & window

  • Data window Dec 25 2025 – Jun 9 2026
  • Total days 167
  • Baseline (P1) 124 days
  • Post-marathon (P2) 43 days
  • Logged workouts 369
  • Marathon date May 3 2026
  • Period split Apr 26 2026

Statistical methods

  • Recovery model OLS multiple regression
  • HRV pathway Pearson + partial r
  • Interventions Cohen's d + t-test
  • Dose-response Quadratic + linear fit
  • Modality Pairwise t-tests
  • Correlations Pearson, threshold 0.5
  • Significance α = 0.05

Caveats & skipped analyses

Post-marathon n=43 is modest — period-2 effect sizes (and the dose-response vertex shift) are descriptive, not prescriptive. The dose-response quadratic never reached significance, so the "optimal strain zone" is not claimed. Modality comparisons all fell short of significance and several post-marathon cells dropped below the n≥8 floor. Intervention behaviors are self-reported journal flags — associations only, not causal. Nutrition/meal-annotation correlation was out of scope this run and is omitted rather than stubbed. All charts are powered by data baked into this file; nothing is fetched at runtime.
